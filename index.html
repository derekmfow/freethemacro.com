What This Actually Is

It's not a meal plan. It's a process.

Most coaches hand you a template before they know a single thing about you. That's the cookie-cutter trap — and it's exactly why everything you've tried stopped working.

Here's how this actually starts: we collect one real week of how you eat — not the ideal, the real. That gives us your baseline and a read on where you are metabolically right now. We ask how meals actually feel: energy, hunger, digestion, how it lines up with your training. Then we dial it in, meal by meal — and we tell you why every time, so it holds long after coaching ends.

I won't guess at you. A generic answer off a five-minute conversation is just another guess — and guessing is the thing that's had you stuck for years. The precision is the product.
